### In SeigManager, the staked TON used as Vote in tally DAO<u> is locked</u>.

- The staked TON used as voting rights are locked and cannot be withdrawn.
- <u>Once the voting rights have been burnt, the lockup is released.</u>
- Vote voting rights cannot be transferred to ownership, but can be delegated to tally governance.
- Since ownership is not transferred, staked TON remain in the user's account and compound interest can also be received.
- Stake TON used ray units (decimal point 27), but voting rights use wei units (decimal point 18). **Amount of voting rights = Amount of staked TON / 1e9**

### Things to reflect on the Simple Stake screen

- **Because ownership is not transferred**, you can view the compound interest of your staked TON as it is now (no change).
- The amount used as voting rights must be shown and the amount that cannot be withdrawn must be indicated.
- You can input the amount from the staked TON and have it mint as a voting right.
- You can burn your voting rights and return them to a state where they can be withdrawn.

# Governor

[Choose Governor parameters | Tally Docs](https://docs.tally.xyz/user-guides/deploying-governor-daos/choose-governor-parameters)

- The **Proposal Threshold : 제안하는 사람의 투표권의 양 **
  - **Governors on Tally usually set the threshold to somewhere between 0% and 2% of circulating token supply.) **
- The Quorum : 정족수, 찬성 투표  양  
  - For Governors on Tally, the quorum is usually somewhere in the range of 1-10% of circulating token supply.
- the Voting Period : how voting lasts on each proposal
  - Most DAOs pick a period between 3 and 7 days. 
- **the Voting Delay : the amount of time between when a proposal is submitted and when it goes up for voting**
  - Most Governors have a voting delay of 0. The Governors that do have one usually pick a period of one day or less.
  - 지연은 토큰 보유자에게 투표가 시작되기 전에 투표권을 위임하거나 더 많은 투표권을 구매할 시간을 줍니다. Governor는 투표 시작 시 모든 투표권의 스냅샷을 찍습니다. 그 후의 위임 및 잔액 변경은 투표권에 영향을 미치지 않습니다. 그렇긴 하지만 대부분의 DAO는 투표 관리를 위한 이 추가 시간이 제안을 늦출 만한 가치가 없다고 결정합니다.
- **the Timelock Delay**
  - the minimum amount of time between when a proposal posses and when it can be executed
  - There’s a big agility vs. security tradeoff here, so DAOs pick values all the way from 0 days to 7 days for their timelock delay.
  - Tally recommends picking a delay of 0 for Governors that only manage a treasury. The security benefit of a delay isn’t as relevant. The exception is if the Timelock has someone with an emergency veto/cancel role. In that case, they probably want at least 3 days to react to potentially malicious proposals.
  - For Governors that control a DeFi protocol  where users might want to withdraw their funds before a big protocol change, we usually see timelock delays closer to 7 days.
- CLOCK_MODE
  - Clock used for flagging checkpoints. : **block number**
- COUNTING_MODE 
  - `quorum=for,abstain` means that both For and Abstain votes are counted towards quorum.
