# Trends in Rollups

## RWA and Stablecoin

- **RWA and Stablecoin Adoption on Ethereum L2**: Projects like Robinhood trading stocks on Arbitrum and stablecoins thriving on Layer 2 solutions highlight the preference for Ethereum’s security while avoiding high L1 costs, driving significant L2 adoption.
- **L1 Revenue Challenges**: Despite Ethereum’s L2 growth, L1 revenue doesn’t directly benefit as fees accrue to L2 rollups, with many projects opting for alternative data availability solutions like Celestia, creating a strategic dilemma.
- **Based Rollup as a Solution ?**: Hegemony through Based Rollup offers a potential fix, aligning L2 scalability with L1’s economic interests by leveraging Ethereum’s native infrastructure for rollup operations.

## Based Rollup

- Core Concept: L2 will not have distinct sequencers. L1 validators do L2 sequencing. Then, L2 will share the same security assumption with L1.
- Related Project: [Taiko](https://taiko.xyz/)
- Proposal Article: [Based rollups—superpowers from L1 sequencing - Layer 2 - Ethereum Research](https://ethresear.ch/t/based-rollups-superpowers-from-l1-sequencing/15016)
- Related Article: [Gorzny et al. "Early Observations of Based Rollups: A Case  Study of Taiko"](https://caaw.io/2025/papers/CAAW25_paper_21.pdf)
- Increasing focus on leveraging Ethereum Layer 1 validators for decentralized transaction sequencing, enhancing scalability and cost-efficiency.
- Growing interest in <u>preconfirmation</u> technologies to improve user experience, as highlighted at recent Ethereum conferences.
- Potential to address fragmentation in the Layer 2 ecosystem by aligning closely with Ethereum's core security.

### Core Protocol

[https://ethcc.io/agenda/the-future-is-based-rollups](https://ethcc.io/agenda/the-future-is-based-rollups)

Summary
Based Rollups are proposed as the future of the Ethereum ecosystem, addressing its current **composition problem** where L2s cannot synchronously process atomic transactions across different networks. The core idea is to enable **synchronous execution** across multiple L2s within a single L1 block, allowing for seamless application composition and reducing fragmentation. This is achieved by **outsourcing L2 sequencing to the L1**, decentralizing the current centralized sequencers.

Based Rollups aim to consolidate liquidity in one place for better pricing and to enhance user experience by enabling single transactions across all L2s. The proposal also suggests **staking L2 tokens** to provide economic security for the network, rather than relying solely on Ethereum's gas fees. While promising, Based Rollups are currently theoretical, facing challenges like the need for L2s to give up MEV revenue and the complexity of engineering implementation.

[https://ethcc.io/agenda/let-validators-do-more](https://ethcc.io/agenda/let-validators-do-more)

Summary
Based Rollups address Ethereum's **fragmentation** and **value capture** issues by decentralizing transaction sequencing. Currently, most rollups rely on centralized sequencers, which contradicts Ethereum's ethos of **decentralization** and **censorship resistance**.

With Based Rollups, **L1 validators** take on a new, crucial role: they directly **sequence rollup transactions**. This reuses Ethereum's powerful validator set as a credibly neutral sequencing layer, extending Ethereum's core values to the rollup layer.

This shift means L1 validators not only secure Ethereum but also actively participate in ordering rollup transactions. As a result, they receive **fees from this sequencing activity**, directly channeling value back to ETH holders and solving the 'value capture' problem.

Technically, this enables **preconfirmations**, offering ultra-fast transaction finality (under 100 milliseconds) for improved user experience. More importantly, it facilitates **seamless composability** between rollups and Ethereum, allowing complex, synchronous cross-rollup transactions. This fosters a more diverse and decentralized rollup ecosystem, aligning with Ethereum's original promise of scaling without compromising its core principles.

### L2 & Rollups

[https://ethcc.io/agenda/state-of-l2s-after-recategorization](https://ethcc.io/agenda/state-of-l2s-after-recategorization)

Summary
L2BEAT, a community watchdog for Ethereum Layer 2 (L2) ecosystems, has recently reclassified L2 chains due to a decline in security standards. **Initially, L2s prioritized data availability (DA) and robust proof systems, but many projects shifted focus to user acquisition, compromising security.**

The reclassification, driven by community feedback, significantly reduced the number of L2s meeting stringent security and decentralization criteria. To qualify, projects must now post data to a DA committee with at least five independent public attesters or directly to Ethereum, and have a fully functional proof system. Optimistic rollups also require open challenge mechanisms or a whitelist of at least five independent validators.

L2BEAT emphasizes that data publishing is crucial for user fund security, contrasting with traditional finance where banks bear this responsibility. The organization operates as a public good, relying on community support to ensure the integrity and security of the L2 ecosystem as it evolves towards real-world financial applications.

[https://ethcc.io/agenda/zk-fy-arbitrum](https://ethcc.io/agenda/zk-fy-arbitrum)

Summary
Offchain Labs' Daniel Lumi introduces Arbitrum's plan to integrate ZK-rollups, offering chains the option to use ZK proving mechanisms alongside their existing fraud proofs. Arbitrum, the largest Layer 2 blockchain, currently relies on fraud proofs, which are cost-effective and highly decentralized, but have a 7-day settlement period.

To address this, Arbitrum is introducing two new options: ZK Proval, a typical ZK rollup for faster settlement, and a hybrid ZK approach that combines the best of both fraud proofs and ZK. The hybrid model offers lower costs and faster settlement by only requiring ZK proofs during disputes.

Arbitrum also emphasizes its multi-prover support, enhancing security and flexibility. Furthermore, its Multi-VM (EVM and WASM) architecture allows developers to use various languages like Rust and C, significantly improving execution efficiency and reducing transaction costs. This strategy aims for long-term sustainability and attracts real-world applications, shifting market preference towards Arbitrum's stack.

### ETC

[https://ethcc.io/agenda/optimizing-commit-and-reveal-for-smart-contracts](https://ethcc.io/agenda/optimizing-commit-and-reveal-for-smart-contracts)

Summary
This presentation discusses the **Commit and Reveal** cryptographic protocol, which allows messages to be hidden and then later revealed. It's crucial for smart contracts to prevent **front-running**, as seen in ENS registrations and auctions.

However, simple Commit and Reveal schemes have limitations, including requiring two rounds, handling unrevealed inputs, and being susceptible to timing games. The research proposes a **delayed execution system** to address these issues, aiming for better front-running protection and capital efficiency in sealed-bid auctions.

This system introduces **causality** and **fairness** properties, using techniques like threshold encryption and time-lock puzzles. While it solves capital inefficiency, it can increase latency and reduce throughput. Implementing this requires careful consideration of Ethereum's existing features and may necessitate a hard fork, though out-of-protocol solutions are also being explored. The presented solution is a prototype, with TEEs and threshold networks offering promising future synergies.
