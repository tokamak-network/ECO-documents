Our decision to provide the DAO to the community as an 'open-source tool' rather than a 'service' is not just a technical choice; it is rooted in a fundamental philosophy of true decentralization and community sovereignty. 

While centralized DAO services may seem convenient, they inherently have the following limitations and undermine the fundamental principles of DAOs: 

**The Quality of Governance: Free Debate vs. Self-Censorship**
The presence of a central entity stifles healthy criticism and debate. A community's growth is driven by active, and at times intense, exchange of opinions and debate. However, on a centralized platform, a service inevitably operates under the control of the service provider. This unconscious self-censorship, driven by questions like, "Is it okay to raise such a critical proposal?" or "Will our discussion be filtered?" becomes the biggest barrier to free communication. The most honest and bold ideas can only emerge in a truly independent space. We aim to guarantee a 'censorship-resistant' environment where the community can freely criticize and debate any entity, including Tokamak Network itself.

**The Potential for Innovation: Open Ecosystem vs. Closed System**
A predefined roadmap allows for only predictable growth. A centralized service confines a community within the limits of its provider's features and roadmap, essentially creating a 'walled garden'. No matter how innovative an idea the community proposes, it cannot be realized if the service provider does not implement it. This deprives the community of the opportunity for autonomous evolution and organic growth.

**The Issue of Sovereignty: True Ownership vs. Limited Right of Use**
A 'limited right of use' is not true ownership. A centralized service offers a well-polished platform to the community. While the community can operate within that space, the ownership of the site, the authority to change rules, and even its very existence depend on the service provider. If the service provider decides to change policies, discontinue the service, or censor certain opinions, the community's autonomy vanishes instantly.

We believe a community should be the 'owner,' not just a 'user.' we are providing a powerful open-source tool that enables communities to determine their own destiny and achieve complete autonomy. This application is a true 'community-owned asset'—one that you can own, run, and modify to fit your specific needs.

### **Introducing the Key Features  **

This web application includes all the core features necessary for successful DAO operations: 

- **On-chain Data Viewing:** Provides transparent, real-time access to all DAO committee data.
- **Comprehensive Proposal Management:** Enables the submission of community proposals, review of proposal lists, and participation in voting by DAO members. Once approved, proposals can be executed by any community member.
- **Committee Member Management:** Supports the management of roles and permissions for committee members, distribution of activity rewards, and participation in proposal voting.
- **Member Challenge Function:** Allows a candidate with more delegated stake than a current member to replace them through the on-chain challenge process.

For a more detailed list of features, please check [**here**](https://github.com/tokamak-network/dao-community-version/blob/main/sample-1/README.md#tokamak-dao-community-version-web-application).
